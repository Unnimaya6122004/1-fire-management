Fire Accident Remedies

  • Call emergency services (911 or your local emergency number) immediately.

  • Alert others in the vicinity and help them evacuate if possible.

  • If your clothes catch fire, stop, drop to the ground, and roll to smother the flames.

  • Stay low to the ground if there is smoke. Smoke rises, so clean air will be closer to the floor.

  • Cover your nose and mouth with a cloth to avoid inhaling smoke.

  • Use the back of your hand to feel doors for heat before opening. If the door is hot, find another way out.

  • If you are unable to escape, seal the room as best as you can (use wet cloths to block smoke) and call for help, letting rescuers know your location.

  • Never use elevators during a fire.

  • Once you are safely outside, do not re-enter the building until it has been declared safe by firefighters.
